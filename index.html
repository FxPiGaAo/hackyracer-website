Hacky Racer

Exploiting Instruction-Level Parallelism to Generate Stealthy Fine-Grained Timers

What is it?

Hacky Racer is a generic timer construction method to facilitate fine-grained information extraction for most side-channel attack, especially those on a restricted environment such as browsers. It exploits and only depends on Instruction-level Parallelism (ILP) from out-of-order CPUs, therefore hard to mitigate.

Specifically, Hacky Racer is built based on two types of gadgets, Racing Gadgets and Magnifier Gadgets, each of which can be utilized individually. Those two gadgets together converts a small timing difference into a theoretically large value. Therefore, any mitigations that try to stop attackers from accessing a fine-grained timer are under an extremely high risk of failure. For example, disabling ShareArryBuffer on the brower alone are now even more incapable to stop any side-channel attack.
